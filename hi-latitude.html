Hi, Latitude!


I spent an embarrassing amount of time poring over the genius that makes up the work boasted on the Latitude website... “Geeking out” is probably putting it gently.

Latitude stands out from the crowd by breaking the mold. In a world where consumers are constantly slapped with an onslaught of ads on every web page they visit, Latitude has found ways to present brands in innovative and unique ways that inspire consumer interaction. By encouraging beauty bloggers to record and share their stories, the “advertisement” becomes a memorable experience for participants at the Ulta booth at BlogHer '13, while also forming a personal bond between the Ulta brand and each blogger.

You guys seem to be experts at connecting consumers with brands in a way that sticks. The Ulta and Sally Hansen campaigns did this by encouraging participation and creating an experience. Even the 90 second “About” video is proof of Latitude's ability to deeply connect a brand and a customer. The video immediately has the viewer identify as the vermillion-colored dot, and just as quickly aligns Latitude by referencing “an agency” in the same shade. Vermillion dots connect the words describing the variety of services Latitude, and by the end of the video, the seed has been planted that by hiring this agency in particular, a company can propel its brand above the rest and reach consumers in ways it hasn't before.

I can't think of a better fit for me than a company that celebrates innovation and boldness, with no fear towards taking on new challenges every day. Yes, I have skills … UX/UI, Illustration, Visual Storytelling, Graphic Design and CNC Laser Engraving to name a few. But more importantly, I have creative talent and a mind that thinks out-of-the-box. And I thrive in high energy, optimistic, future-focused environments where new challenges are embraced with enthusiasm and resourcefulness.

Most of the work in my Portfolio is UX/UI focused. I most recently finished working on Happy Hour, an app design that allows users to record wines and beers they have enjoyed, discover new bottles, and share their wine and beer wishlist with family and friends. I conducted user research, created user stories and user flows, designed wireframes, and fleshed out the finished HD screens, which I used to create a clickable prototype. To see this project and more of the stuff I've worked on, head over to my Portfolio.

Regardless of whether you're hiring right now, I would love the opportunity to sit down and make a personal connection with someone from Latitude, and hear more about the awesome work you guys are doing.

Looking forward to hearing from you!

Sincerely,
Haley Guilfoile
