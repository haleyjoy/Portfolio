Tools



Project

For over 20 years, Heritage Auctions employees made use of 150+ "legacy" applications, many of which were built for a team of 2 or 3 employees to use. With no central program to access data, employees scrambled between 2, 3, sometimes even 7 different programs to handle a client services phone call, or to properly generate an invoice. Teams throughout the company were constantly behind on deadlines due to cumbersome technology that took too long to use. A more robust, modern, and user friendly solution was sorely overdue.
Nexus is a permission-based centralized application designed to empower employees to handle their workload within one application, and not only meet deadlines, but exceed manager expectations. Nexus includes tools that digitize processes that once scraped by on sticky notes and Excel spreadsheets. Outdated data and cumbersome workflows are replaced by live data, an alert system, an automated worklist, and more. Using an updated management system, team leads and managers can save 10+ hours a week that were once spent writing stale data on whiteboards and divying up work.



My Role

At Heritage, I took on the task of designing tools for Collectibles Catalogers across 16 different departments throughout the company, and facilitating their move from their legacy applications to Nexus to ensure adoption. My responsibilities included initial research and observation, stakeholder and user interviews, user case studies, wireframing, code test design, QA testing, stakeholder testing, training / launch, and followup.



Goal

Our goal is to empower all 650+ employees with newly designed tools in Nexus, effectively replacing the legacy applications, and see that all departments are regularly meeting their deadlines.



Process

Discovery / Strategy

Step one of my design process, regardless of the project or goal, is always to drop my ego entirely. The users I would be working on designing new tools for are world experts on their respective categories, so in order to design the most effective software, I need to learn about their processes and their unique needs.

With departments like US Coins, Sports Memorabilia, Collectible Comic Books, Luxury Real Estate, and more, each department has vastly different requirements, challenges, and business rules. However, since Operations team members and Client Services representatives would also be accessing these pages, so uniformity would be key to ensure that even those employees who arent industry experts would still be able to quickly identify key information with ease.
